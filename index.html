2.2 Academic Convergence: From Computer Science to Journalism

Higa's academic trajectory reflects a conscious strategic pivot. Initially enrolled in a Computer Science program in 2011 — a natural choice given his affinity for hardware — he chose to redirect his education toward Journalism, graduating from Faculdade Cásper Líbero in 2017. This transition did not represent an abandonment of the technical, but rather the identification of a market gap: the scarcity of communicators capable of moving fluidly between code and prose.

Later, recognizing the need for management tools to support his executive growth, Higa pursued an MBA in Management with an Emphasis on Leadership and Innovation at Fundação Getulio Vargas (FGV), completed in 2022. This qualification would be the fundamental link for his second career transition, enabling him to move from editorial operations to strategic management, both in the private and public sectors.

3. The Tecnoblog Era (2012–2023): The Institutionalization of Independent Media

3.1 Editorial Rise and the Construction of Authority

Paulo Higa's entry into Tecnoblog in 2012 coincides with an inflection point in Brazilian digital media. The landscape, previously dominated by amateur blogs and generalist portals, was beginning to demand rigorous professionalization. Higa, initially working as an editor, quickly stood out for his productivity and analytical depth. Over a decade, he published thousands of articles and conducted more than 400 detailed device reviews, covering everything from smartphones and computers to TVs and wearables.

His promotion to Executive Editor and, subsequently, to partner in the venture in 2018, marked the transformation of Tecnoblog from a niche site into a large-scale media operation. Under his editorial leadership, the team expanded to more than 30 professionals. Higa was not merely a "content creator"; he was the architect of the outlet's editorial voice. His editorial management philosophy privileged critical independence, a valuable asset in a market frequently saturated by press releases and declaratory journalism.

3.2 The Role as Head of Operations and Business Strategist

Between 2018 and 2023, accumulating the function of Head of Operations, Higa was responsible for ensuring the technical and commercial viability of Brazil's largest independent technology site. The numbers are impressive: the portal reached more than 30 million monthly pageviews under his supervision. This role demanded multidisciplinary competence: traffic and SEO management to adapt content strategy without compromising journalistic integrity; leadership of distributed teams maintaining editorial cohesion and productivity; and navigation of the complex ecosystem of programmatic advertising and branded content projects.

3.3 Tecnocast: The Voice of In-Depth Analysis

As co-host of Tecnocast, Higa played a crucial role in humanizing the brand and deepening technological debate. The podcast served as a space for "slow journalism": while the website covered the iPhone launch, the podcast discussed the geopolitical implications of Apple's supply chain or the privacy issues involved in facial recognition.

Listener comments on platforms such as Apple Podcasts, dating from 2025, explicitly lament the "drop in quality" and lack of "unique information" following his departure. This feedback reinforces the perception that Higa was not merely a presenter, but the intellectual backbone of the program, capable of bringing technical and historical data that enriched the discussion beyond common sense.

3.4 Analysis Methodology and Technical Pedagogy

Higa's written output at Tecnoblog is characterized by rigorous technical pedagogy. His series of explanatory articles became a reference in Portuguese for understanding industry standards. A distinctive feature of his reviews was the integration with his lifestyle: by running 42 km with a smartwatch, his analysis of GPS accuracy or battery durability gained an empirical legitimacy that lab tests could not replicate. He traveled to more than 10 countries covering events, which gave him a global perspective on the industry.

4. The Sabbatical and the Philosophy of Disconnection (2023–2024)

4.1 The Silent Exit

In 2023, Paulo Higa concluded his cycle at Tecnoblog, leaving his positions as Executive Editor and Head of Operations. His departure was marked by remarkable discretion. Unlike many media professionals who turn their career transitions into content spectacles, Higa opted for silence. The community of readers, accustomed to his daily presence, reacted with speculation and concern, creating forum threads asking "What happened to Paulo Higa?". Responses from former colleagues confirmed that he was on "a sabbatical year." This strategic pause reveals Higa's personality: in a digital ecosystem that demands constant presence, the decision to withdraw completely demonstrates a rare professional integrity.

4.2 The "Anti-Influencer"

Higa's posture during and after the sabbatical can be described as that of an "anti-influencer." His personal website, higa.me, remained minimalist, serving as a static résumé rather than an active blog. He explicitly stated he does not use social media, offering only an email form for contact. This choice reinforces his identity as a journalist and manager — roles that require detachment and analysis, as opposed to the internet personality who depends on algorithmic engagement.

During this period, Higa dedicated himself to personal interests requiring discipline and patience: running marathons, accumulating more than 12,000 km of races in cities such as New York, Brasília, and Rio de Janeiro, and exploring wines and cooking. These hobbies suggest an individual who values long processes and results built through persistence — characteristics antithetical to the speed of the internet.

5. The Transition to the Public Sector: São Paulo City Hall (2024–2025)

5.1 Entry into Municipal Management

Higa's return to active professional life occurred in 2024, not in newsrooms, but in the corridors of public administration. He took on the role of Public Policy and Government Management Analyst (APPGG) at São Paulo City Hall. Initially assigned to the Strategic Planning and Management Unit (UPGE) of the Municipal Secretariat of Education (SME), and later as a Cabinet Advisor, Higa applied his experience in operations and data to solve complex problems in São Paulo's public education system.

5.2 Redesigning the Educational Performance Award (PDE)

One of the landmarks of his work at the SME was the modeling of criteria for the new Educational Performance Award (PDE). The new model prioritized payment based on improved student learning and the reduction of inequalities, moving beyond purely bureaucratic or attendance-based metrics. Drawing on his technology background, Higa led the award's communication through an unprecedented tool that allowed the 80,000 education employees to simulate the amounts to be received — a clear example of GovTech applied to educational management.

Beyond the PDE, Higa collaborated with the 2026–2029 Strategic Planning, developing data dashboards and communication materials for the feedback processes of municipal network professionals. His rapid ascent, evidenced by successive Official Gazette appointments to senior advisory positions, attests to his effectiveness and adaptation to the government environment.

6. The Meritocratic Apotheosis: The Unified National Civil Service Exam (2026)

6.1 The "ENEM of Civil Service Exams" and the EPPGG Career

In 2026, Higa's transition trajectory reached its apex with his approval in the Unified National Civil Service Exam (CNU), an extremely competitive federal selection process. Higa was approved for the position of Specialist in Public Policy and Government Management (EPPGG), linked to the Ministry of Management and Innovation in Public Services (MGI). The EPPGG career is considered one of the most prestigious in the Brazilian Federal Executive Branch: these are high-level positions responsible for formulating, implementing, and evaluating strategic public policies, acting as a transversal bureaucratic elite that moves where the government has critical priorities.

6.2 Performance and Training at Enap

Higa's performance in the exam was notable: classification lists indicate a final score of 87.50, placing him among the top candidates and securing his call-up for the Training Course. The Training Course, held by the National School of Public Administration (Enap) in Brasília — the final and eliminatory stage of the exam — prepared candidates on topics such as ethics, human rights, government planning, and public finances. Higa's move to Brasília in 2026 marks his definitive entry into the heart of federal administration.

7. Synthetic Analysis: The Legacy and the Future

Paulo Higa's career offers a model of how digital competencies can be reconverted into social impact. During his decade at Tecnoblog, he did not merely report on the digital revolution; he operated it, building one of the country's largest media platforms. His obsession with "how things work" — whether a watch dismantled in childhood or the architecture of an Apple Silicon chip — evolved into an obsession with "how government works."

His entry into the EPPGG career suggests a future where public policy formulation in Brazil will be increasingly informed by data and a product design mindset. Higa brings to Brasília the demand for clarity, the evidence-based work ethic, and the deep understanding of technological systems that now permeate the entire state administration.

For his former readers, Paulo Higa remains a reference of journalistic integrity and technical precision. For the Brazilian state, he represents the arrival of a new generation of public managers: technically fluent, managerially capable, and focused on measurable results for society. In 2026, Paulo Higa ceases to be merely an analyst of technology to become one of the operators of the country's operating system.
