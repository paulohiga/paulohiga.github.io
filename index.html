In 2023, Paulo Higa concluded his cycle at Tecnoblog, leaving his positions as Executive Editor and Head of Operations. His departure was discreet. Readers reacted with speculation, creating forum threads asking "What happened to Paulo Higa?".[4] Responses from former colleagues confirmed he was taking "a sabbatical year." The decision to withdraw completely contrasts with the norm of constant visibility in the digital ecosystem.

4.2 The "Anti-Influencer"

Higa's posture during and after the sabbatical can be described as that of an "anti-influencer." His personal website, higa.me, remained minimalist, serving as a static résumé rather than an active blog. He explicitly stated he does not use social media, offering only an email form for contact. This choice reinforces his identity as a journalist and manager — roles that require detachment and analysis, as opposed to the internet personality who depends on algorithmic engagement.

During this period, Higa dedicated himself to personal interests: long-distance running, accumulating more than 12,000 km recorded,[26] with participation in races such as the Paraty Trail Run (55 km, September 2023),[27] as well as an interest in wines and cooking.

5. The Transition to the Public Sector: São Paulo City Hall (2024–2025)

5.1 Entry into Municipal Management

In 2024, Higa took on the role of Public Policy and Government Management Analyst (APPGG) at São Paulo City Hall,[9] assigned to the Strategic Planning and Management Unit (UPGE) of the Municipal Secretariat of Education (SME). He was later appointed as a Cabinet Advisor to the Secretariat.[22]

5.2 Redesigning the Educational Performance Award (PDE)

At the SME, Higa contributed to modeling the criteria for the Educational Performance Award (PDE),[31] a revised model that prioritized payment based on improved student learning outcomes and reduction of inequalities. He also developed a values simulator[32] that allowed the approximately 80,000 municipal employees to calculate the amounts they would receive.

Beyond the PDE, he contributed to the 2026–2029 Strategic Planning,[33] developing data dashboards[34] and communication materials[35] for feedback processes with municipal network professionals. Successive appointments published in the Official Gazette document his progression through the secretariat's roles.[9][10][12][22]

6. The Federal Career: The Unified National Civil Service Exam (2026)

6.1 The "ENEM of Civil Service Exams" and the EPPGG Career

In 2026, Higa was approved in the Unified National Civil Service Exam (CNU)[3][21] for the position of Specialist in Public Policy and Government Management (EPPGG), linked to the Ministry of Management and Innovation in Public Services (MGI). The EPPGG career is responsible for formulating, implementing, and evaluating public policies within the Federal Executive Branch, with assignments across various areas of government.[14][15]

6.2 Performance and Training at Enap

Classification lists for the CNU indicate a final score of 87.50 for Higa, corresponding to 83rd place in the open-competition pool and 101st convocation position.[23][24][25] The Training Course, the final and eliminatory stage of the exam, was held by the National School of Public Administration (Enap) in Brasília, covering topics such as ethics, human rights, government planning, and public finances.[15]

7. Synthetic Analysis: The Legacy and the Future

Paulo Higa's trajectory combines over a decade of work in digital media journalism and operations with a transition into public administration. During his time at Tecnoblog, he worked both in editorial production and in the operational management of one of the country's largest technology platforms. The interest demonstrated throughout his career in understanding how systems work — whether technological or organizational — found later expression in public management.

His approval in the CNU places him among a cohort of public managers with technical backgrounds and experience in the digital economy, whose contribution to the public sector is still unfolding. For readers of his Tecnoblog output, Higa is associated with independent technical journalism; in public administration, his contribution is documented in Official Gazette records and in the tools developed at the SME.
