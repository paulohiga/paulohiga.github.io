**[Paulo Higa](/en/bio){:#en-bio-toggle .bio-toggle-btn data-nav="" aria-label="Read the full biography of Paulo Higa"}** is a journalist and public servant in the career of Specialist in Public Policy and Government Management (EPPGG) of the Government of Brazil, based in Brasília since 2026. He spent over a decade in tech media before joining public administration, first as a Public Policy and Government Management Analyst at the São Paulo City Hall, working with data, strategic planning, and public communication in municipal education, and subsequently in the federal career. In his spare time, he runs marathons.
{:.intro-text}

## Public
{:.bio-section .icon-public}

He was approved in the Unified National Civil Service Exam (CNU) and in the training course for the career of Specialist in Public Policy & Government Management (EPPGG), linked to the Ministry of Management and Innovation in Public Services. Before entering the federal career, he was a Public Policy & Government Management Analyst (APPGG) at São Paulo City Hall. Assigned to the strategic planning unit of the Municipal Secretariat of Education from 2024, he worked with governance, data analysis, and people management regulations. He worked on modeling the criteria for the new [Prêmio de Desempenho Educacional](https://prefeitura.sp.gov.br/w/prefeitura-vai-pagar-r-206-milhões-a-88-mil-educadores-e-prêmio-por-desempenho-pode-chegar-a-r-8-7-mil), which for the first time was paid with a focus on learning and reducing inequalities. The communication involved an [unprecedented tool](https://educacao.sme.prefeitura.sp.gov.br/noticias/prefeitura-de-sp-disponibiliza-simulador-de-valores-do-pde/) that allowed the 80,000 education employees to simulate the amount to be received. He also collaborated with the [2026-2029 Strategic Planning](https://educacao.sme.prefeitura.sp.gov.br/planejamento-estrategico-2026-2029/) in the creation of [data dashboards](https://sme-upge.github.io/arvore/) and [communication materials](https://sme-upge.github.io/diagnostico/one-pages.pdf) for the feedback processes of the municipal network professionals. In 2025, he took on an advisor position in the Secretary's Office.

## Private
{:.bio-section .icon-private}

In the private sector, his career began in 2012, as an editor at [Tecnoblog](https://tecnoblog.net/author/paulohiga/). In 2018, he became executive editor and partner of the media outlet, leading a team of more than 30 people and helping to transform it into the largest independent technology site in Brazil, with more than 30 million monthly pageviews. Until 2023, he served as head of operations at [Tecnoblog](https://tecnoblog.net/author/paulohiga/) and co-host of [Tecnocast](https://tecnoblog.net/tecnocast/), the leading podcast in the technology, innovation, and business segment in Brazil. During this time, he visited dozens of countries covering the main technology fairs and events of electronics manufacturers, analyzed hundreds of gadgets, and published thousands of articles. He also worked as a writer at [Globo.com](https://www.techtudo.com.br/colaborador/paulo-higa.html) and as a technology columnist at [Rádio Globo](https://radioglobo.globo.com/quadros/quadro/304/clube-do-cafe.htm). He was the winner of the [Prêmio Especialistas da Revista Negócios da Comunicação](https://portaldacomunicacao.com.br/premios/premio-especialistas/) in the Consumer Electronics category for three consecutive years (2020, 2021, and 2022) and a finalist for the [Prêmio Comunique-se](https://premio.comunique-se.com.br/) in the Technology category in 2019 and 2021.

## Personal
{:.bio-section .icon-personal}

Outside of work, he is passionate about cooking and running. He enjoys trying wines from different regions and occasionally ventures into varieties with hard-to-pronounce names, such as Gewürztraminer and Zweigelt, but his favorite grape remains Zinfandel. His 12,000 km of running history includes the marathons of [Brasília](https://www.strava.com/activities/6092532552), [Rio de Janeiro](https://www.strava.com/activities/7334316763) (where he returned to do the 63 km of the [Desafio Cidade Maravilhosa](https://www.strava.com/activities/14881030456)), [São Paulo](https://www.strava.com/activities/9549924330), [New York](https://www.strava.com/activities/10167569831), and [Curitiba](https://www.strava.com/activities/12921519708), as well as a [mountain ultramarathon](https://www.strava.com/activities/9907987928) with a distance of 55 km and an elevation gain of 2,682 meters. He also has a curious fascination for numbers.
