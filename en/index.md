**[Paulo Higa](/en/bio){:#en-bio-toggle .bio-toggle-btn data-nav="" aria-label="Read the full biography of Paulo Higa"}** is a journalist and public servant in the Specialist in Public Policy and Government Management (EPPGG) career of the Government of Brazil, based in Brasília since 2026. He spent over a decade in tech media before moving into public administration, first as a Public Policy and Government Management Analyst at São Paulo City Hall — working with data, strategic planning, and public communication in municipal education — and later in the federal civil service. In his spare time, he runs marathons.
{:.intro-text}

## Public
{:.bio-section .icon-public}

He passed the Unified National Civil Service Exam (CNU) and the training course for the Specialist in Public Policy and Government Management (EPPGG) career, which is linked to the Ministry of Management and Innovation in Public Services. Before joining the federal civil service, he was a Public Policy and Government Management Analyst (APPGG) at São Paulo City Hall. Assigned to the strategic planning unit of the Municipal Secretariat of Education from 2024 onward, he worked on governance, data analysis, and human resources regulations. He helped model the criteria for the new [Prêmio de Desempenho Educacional](https://prefeitura.sp.gov.br/w/prefeitura-vai-pagar-r-206-milhões-a-88-mil-educadores-e-prêmio-por-desempenho-pode-chegar-a-r-8-7-mil), which for the first time was awarded with a focus on learning and on reducing inequalities. The rollout included a [first-of-its-kind tool](https://educacao.sme.prefeitura.sp.gov.br/noticias/prefeitura-de-sp-disponibiliza-simulador-de-valores-do-pde/) that let the 80,000 education employees simulate the amount they would receive. He also contributed to the [2026-2029 Strategic Planning](https://educacao.sme.prefeitura.sp.gov.br/planejamento-estrategico-2026-2029/), building [data dashboards](https://sme-upge.github.io/arvore/) and [communication materials](https://sme-upge.github.io/diagnostico/one-pages.pdf) for the listening sessions held with the municipal network's professionals. In 2025, he took on an advisory role in the Secretary's Office.

## Private
{:.bio-section .icon-private}

In the private sector, his career began in 2012 as an editor at [Tecnoblog](https://tecnoblog.net/author/paulohiga/). In 2018, he became executive editor and a partner in the outlet, leading a team of more than 30 people and helping to turn it into the largest independent technology website in Brazil, with more than 30 million monthly pageviews. Until 2023, he served as head of operations at [Tecnoblog](https://tecnoblog.net/author/paulohiga/) and co-host of [Tecnocast](https://tecnoblog.net/tecnocast/), Brazil's leading podcast on technology, innovation, and business. Over that period, he traveled to dozens of countries to cover the major technology trade shows and electronics manufacturers' events, reviewed hundreds of gadgets, and published thousands of articles. He also worked as a writer for [Globo.com](https://www.techtudo.com.br/colaborador/paulo-higa.html) and as a technology columnist on [Rádio Globo](https://radioglobo.globo.com/quadros/quadro/304/clube-do-cafe.htm). He won the [Prêmio Especialistas da Revista Negócios da Comunicação](https://portaldacomunicacao.com.br/premios/premio-especialistas/) in the Consumer Electronics category for three consecutive years (2020, 2021, and 2022) and was a finalist for the [Prêmio Comunique-se](https://premio.comunique-se.com.br/) in the Technology category in 2019 and 2021.

## Personal
{:.bio-section .icon-personal}

Beyond his professional career, he is passionate about cooking and running. He enjoys trying wines from different regions and occasionally ventures into varieties with hard-to-pronounce names, such as Gewürztraminer and Zweigelt, though his favorite grape remains Zinfandel. Among his 12,000 km of running are the marathons of [Brasília](https://www.strava.com/activities/6092532552), [Rio de Janeiro](https://www.strava.com/activities/7334316763) (where he came back to tackle the 63 km of the [Desafio Cidade Maravilhosa](https://www.strava.com/activities/14881030456)), [São Paulo](https://www.strava.com/activities/9549924330), [New York](https://www.strava.com/activities/10167569831), and [Curitiba](https://www.strava.com/activities/12921519708), as well as a 55 km [mountain ultramarathon](https://www.strava.com/activities/9907987928) with 2,682 meters of elevation gain. He also has a curious fascination with numbers.
